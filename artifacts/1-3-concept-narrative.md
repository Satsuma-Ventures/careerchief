# Meritous — Concept Narrative
*The story, told cold — for sharing before anyone sees a screen. Phase 3 · 2026-07-17*

## In one breath
You've done far more than your résumé says — but you're too close to see it, and a blank page never asks the right question. **Meritous is the partner that draws your real story out, one question at a time — so you sound like you, not what some aggregated AI thinks you should sound like.**

## The problem
Every few years you have to explain your career — a résumé, a promotion case, an interview. And every time, a decade of judgment collapses into a few flat lines. *"Managed vendor relationships."* It isn't false; it's just so much smaller than the truth. We're too close to our own work to see its weight, and no blank page asks the question that would draw it out. So the most important things you've done stay unsaid.

## Why it's getting worse, not better
The obvious fix is "use AI." But generic AI does the opposite of what you need: it works from everyone's data, so it regresses you toward the average — a competent, forgettable paragraph that sounds like everybody else. Right when it matters most to sound like *you*, the tools make you sound like the mean.

## The insight
The bottleneck was never the writing. It's knowing what to **ask**. A great career coach doesn't write your story — they pull it out of you with the one sharp question you'd never think to answer. That skill — elicitation — is what we've built. Not a résumé generator: a partner that knows what to ask.

## The moment — this is the whole thing
Give it one flat line: *"I managed the vendor relationships."* Instead of a signup wall, your partner asks: *"Take me to the moment it nearly went wrong — who did the room turn to, and what did you decide that no one else could?"* And the real story lands on the table: the overnight supplier save that held a product launch together. Still true — just no longer undersold. **That happens in ten seconds, before you've made an account.**

## What it becomes
Line by line, that grows into a living **Career Brief** you own — the whole arc of your work, in your words, every claim traceable to something you actually said. It shows you exactly where you're underselling yourself, and re-threads that same true story toward the role you're reaching for. It represents you when you're not in the room.

## The frame
You hold the pen. The partner just helps you find the words. AI, finally working *for* you — from your history alone — so you become **more yourself, not less.**

# Gate Brief — Track 1 / Phase 3

*Date: 2026-07-17 · Venture: Meritous (repo: careerchief) · Skill: 3-concept v2.2*

## What we found

Phase 3 produced a design system and five concept screens for **Meritous** — the AI **career partner** that excavates a mid-career professional's real experience and re-threads it (résumé, positioning, interview prep) for the level they're targeting.

What surprised us: the design took several rounds to escape a generic "AI-generated" register, and the unlock wasn't more prose direction — it was **tokenized reference specs** (Refero) plus a decisive type/accent system. What *held up* from the thesis: the wedge is **elicitation** (knowing what to ask), and it's demonstrable in a live pre-login moment; the **Career Brief** as an owned, compounding, fully-traceable asset answers the stickiness risk; the **extract → reflect → sign-off** interaction keeps the user as the author. What *emerged*: a named **partner cast** differentiated by domain and temperament, a pre-login "find your partner + one line" on-ramp aimed at cold-start, and a **personal-career-site** adjacency worth exploring.

## Three strongest signals

1. **The wedge demonstrates, it doesn't describe.** The pre-login excavation — one flat line → the sharp question → your real story reframed but still true, in ten seconds — makes *"it knows what to ask"* felt, not claimed. It's the sharpest differentiator and doubles as the activation on-ramp against the #1 risk.
2. **The stickiness answer is designed in.** The Career Brief as an owned, living, fully-traceable document — every claim grounded in the user's own words ("nothing here is invented") — turns an episodic need into a compounding asset, and the grounding is both moat and trust mechanism.
3. **The brand escaped the generic-AI trap.** The all-serif editorial system (Literata voice + Hanken chrome), warm paper, gold-as-earned reads as considered and human — serving the "companion, not tool" thesis and differentiating hard from every sans-serif AI competitor.

## Three highest risks

1. **Communicability / time-to-understanding (founder-flagged).** The concept is sophisticated; handed cold, it's a lot to absorb. Value must be legible fast or the funnel never starts. Mitigations begun — the interactive moment (show, don't tell), the landing trim, the concept narrative — but this is the top thing to validate with real cold users.
2. **Everything hinges on the elicitation actually being good.** Every mock shows a great question. If the real product's questions are generic, the differentiation collapses to "another résumé tool." The wedge is an execution bet on AI quality + encoded methodology, unproven until built.
3. **B2C economics + retention-after-goal.** Activation is addressed; sustained retention (career need is episodic) and CAC/LTV are not proven by a concept. The always-on "representative" horizon is the hoped-for answer, but it's direction, not v1 scope.

## Design & product notes for Phase 5

- **Partner personalities:** house voice + six temperaments defined (CONTENT.md §7); full per-partner scripting (question calibration, follow-ups) is Phase 5. Decide launch breadth — all six, or start with 2–3.
- **Privacy = product + business model:** the "never sold / never trained on / always yours" promises are real commitments and a likely differentiator/pricing lever. Decide the data + auth model.
- **Pre-login mechanics:** how much free value before the wall; whether the anonymous first answer persists into the account.
- **Marketing surface:** `meetmeritous.com` is the home for imagery and the cold pitch — kept separate from the type-led product screens. The **shareable personal-career-site** (the Career Brief as a public page) is an adjacent product worth scoping.
- **Sustainability/impact** as a deliberate niche wedge (aligns with the studio impact thesis) — a positioning decision, not a default domain.
- **Production gaps:** logos are font-rendered and need outlining to vector paths; the gap-avatar initial is hardcoded in CSS and must be data-driven; a few component sizes remain hardcoded vs. tokens.
- **Tooling:** trial the **Refero MCP** as a standard Phase 3 input (studio brief at `studio/explorations/refero-design-mcp.md`).

## Recommendation

**Go — commit to Track 2.** The Signal Brief holds up, the thesis is believable, and the concept is compelling and differentiated. The two things that *must* be true — the elicitation is genuinely good, and cold users get it fast — should be the first things Track 2 de-risks (a real elicitation prototype + cold-user tests), not assumptions carried silently forward.

# Concept Brief: Meritous
*Date: 2026-07-08 · revised 2026-07-16 · Skill: 3-concept v2.2*

## What these screens need to demonstrate

The overarching experience these screens must create: **the ideal professional cofounder, applied to your career.** Someone who picks you up when you're down. Who sees what you can't see about yourself. Who speaks candidly because they're unambiguously in your corner — not performing support, but genuinely invested in a shared goal. Who knows exactly when to push for more rather than let you settle. Not a coach running a protocol. Not a tool. A partner with skin in the game.

Within that experience, four proof points must land beyond "nice UI":
1. **The methodology is the product** — the sharp, non-obvious question a blank AI wouldn't think to ask. This is the wedge; it must be visible on screen, not described in copy. And it should feel like a Career Partner asking it — because they already see you more clearly than you see yourself, and they're not going to let you undersell it.
2. **The corpus is a visibly growing, user-owned asset** — the Career Brief is something that compounds and travels with you, engineering the stickiness the thesis flags as the #1 risk. Your Career Partner holds your professional story as well as you do — better in some ways. This screen makes that visible.
3. **The relationship is high-touch and durable** — not a one-and-done generator. A partner who remembers you, holds your story, and can represent you even when you're not in the room — gesturing at the always-on "sports agent / representative" horizon.
4. **The value is felt before the signup wall** — the unauthenticated homepage demonstrates the wedge live and *starts* onboarding before auth, so activation begins with a taste of the real thing, not an empty form. This is the B2C on-ramp and the concept's answer to the cold-start / activation risk.

These screens exist to support a **commit-or-kill gate decision**, so they must make the value legible fast — not represent a complete product.

## Non-negotiable — this must NOT look or feel like a generic SaaS app

Meritous is, structurally, a SaaS product — but it must never read as one. Every screen holds the **warm, editorial, scholarly** register of the design system: Libertinus Serif throughout, warm ivory paper, generous space, brass reserved for genuinely earned milestones only. The reference is a **considered publication or a private-client advisory**, not a project-management tool.

Actively avoid the SaaS defaults that will creep in — especially on the landing page and the app views:
- **No** dashboard-of-widgets or metric-tile grids
- **No** tracked-out uppercase micro-labels (the templated-AI/SaaS tell — the design system uses sentence-case serif-italic eyebrows instead)
- **No** feature-card marketing grid, logo-cloud, or pricing-tier table energy on the landing page
- **No** chat-bubble/typing-dot conversation UI; dialogue is set in the display serif as a skilled interview
- **No** AI-sparkle, gradients, or "✨ AI-powered" copy — capability is demonstrated, never announced

When a layout decision defaults to "the SaaS way," choose the **editorial** way instead.

## Screen inventory (5 screens)

### Screen 1 — Landing page (the unauthenticated homepage)
**What it shows:** The public homepage a cold visitor reaches from word-of-mouth, SEO, or an ad — the B2C conversion surface. It states the value proposition in the cofounder register (never "AI résumé tool"), gives a quiet glimpse of the named **Career Partner** cast (the people you'll actually work with), and — the centerpiece — an **embedded live excavation moment**: the visitor drops in one flat, undersold line about their experience, and Meritous replies inline with the single sharp question a blank AI wouldn't ask. No signup wall to feel the magic. A calm handoff — "keep going, and we'll build the whole brief" — carries their answer into signup.
**Why this screen:** For a B2C venture this is the most important surface for the gate — it's where a cold visitor decides the value is real. It demonstrates the wedge instead of describing it, gives value before asking for anything, and starts onboarding pre-auth — directly attacking the thesis's #1 risk (cold-start / activation friction). If the homepage doesn't make someone lean in, the funnel never starts.
**Key elements:** An editorial hero (serif display, generous space, warm ivory — reads like the cover of a considered publication, not a SaaS landing template); a value line in the cofounder voice; a restrained introduction to the Career Partner cast; and the inline "try it" excavation field returning a real, specific question. A single primary CTA that reads as *continuing* ("Pick up where you left off"), not "Sign up free." No feature grid, no logo cloud, no pricing tiers.

### Screen 2 — The excavation conversation (the wedge, at depth)
**What it shows:** The conversation continued — the *same thread* that began with the pre-login question, now in the full authenticated session. The user gave a flat description ("I managed the vendor relationships"); the Career Partner responds not with generic follow-up but with a *sharp, specific* elicitation move that surfaces buried scope, stakes, and outcome the user assumed was obvious — and reflects back the reframed, still-truthful version. A subtle cue shows the pre-login answer already carried in, and the exchange being captured into the growing brief.
**Why this screen:** This is *the* differentiator — and the moment the cofounder relationship is most viscerally felt. The question didn't come from a protocol. It came from someone who already sees the user more clearly than they see themselves, and isn't going to let them undersell it. It proves the encoded methodology ("knowing what to ask") against the "blank ChatGPT can't do this" claim, and proves the seamless activation on-ramp — the pre-login taste becomes the real thing with no cold restart.
**Key elements:** Conversation thread in second-person voice, dialogue set in the display serif (per the "skilled interview, not a chat widget" rule) — no bubbles, no typing dots. The elicitation question styled with candid authority — confident and direct, not bubbly, not clinical. A cue that the session *continues* from the pre-login moment. An inline "captured to your brief" affordance. Calm, high-focus layout. The tone is: your Career Partner just asked "wait — what was the actual impact there?"

### Screen 3 — The Career Brief (the living, owned corpus)
**What it shows:** The primary output artifact — a living document capturing the user's narrative, strengths, and positioning, assembled from the conversation. Each element is **traceable** back to what the user said (a visible "grounded in your own words" cue). It reads as a considered, editorial document the user *owns and can export*, not a form. A quiet cue signals it keeps growing over time and can later represent the user to inbound opportunity (the "agent" / representative horizon).
**Why this screen:** Your Career Partner holds your professional story as well as you do — better in some ways. They can walk into any room and represent you, even when you're not there. This screen makes that tangible: a living document that compounds, travels with you, and speaks on your behalf. It's the stickiness answer the thesis demands be designed in from the first screen.
**Key elements:** Editorial document layout (serif headings, generous space), strengths/positioning sections, traceability cues that visibly anchor every claim to the user's real words. An explicit **user-owned / exportable** affordance. A subtle "living / always-on" framing — the document grows as the relationship deepens. Brass reserved for a genuine milestone marker only.

### Screen 4 — Story Score & Gap Detection (the value-creation delta)
**What it shows:** The signal metric reflecting narrative coherence/completeness, broken into sub-dimensions (narrative clarity, keyword alignment, impact quantification), paired with **Gap Detection** — the specific delta between what the user has actually done and how they're currently presenting it, with concrete, honest prompts to close each gap.
**Why this screen:** This is the Career Partner's candor made visible. Not flattery — never "you're 98% perfect!" — and not harshness. The honest read from someone genuinely invested in your outcome: "here's where you're leaving value on the table, and here's what I actually see when I look at your history." The gap list is the partner holding a mirror. Demonstrates the core value mechanism as *excavation, not generation* — naming what's missing and undersold rather than fabricating.
**Key elements:** Score card (the one disciplined use of brass — an earned achievement moment, not decoration), sub-dimension breakdown, a prioritized gap list with specific, grounded next actions. Tone is candid and direct — the kind of feedback that only lands because you trust the source is in your corner.

### Screen 5 — Role tailoring & interview prep (re-threading for the target level)
**What it shows:** The user points the Career Brief at a specific target role; Meritous re-threads the *same real history* into targeted outputs — a tailored résumé angle and role-specific interview prep grounded in the user's own stories, positioned for the level they're reaching for (not the one they're leaving).
**Why this screen:** A Career Partner knows your whole history. When you say "I'm going after this," they reach into what they already know about you and aim it — the same real stories, reframed for where you're headed. "You've done this before, just in a different context." Proves re-threading, closes the excavation → outcome loop, and shows the corpus paying off repeatedly — the compounding-relationship behavior that separates a subscription that stays from a one-and-done transaction.
**Key elements:** Target-role input/context, tailored output grounded in cited real experience, interview-prep questions tied to the user's actual stories. A "same story, aimed higher" framing — the partner who helps you see your own history through the lens of where you're going. Outputs visibly traceable — never generic template filler.

## Signature motif to explore — the held line ("a line held for you")

The design system's identity already carries a **held line** — the wordmark's brass underscore, and the `.held-line` / `.held-blank` components (a quiet hairline while empty/waiting, turning brass only when earned/filled/signed). **Explore extending it into a unifying convention across all five screens.** It's worth trying because it does three things at once:
- **Résumé-world grounding without being a résumé builder** — a ruled line reads as editorial / manuscript / ledger and as the signature line of a considered document, not a form field.
- **The narrative of potential** — the empty line is the blank about your future the partner is holding space for; filling it is the excavation giving you the words. *A line held for you.*
- **A consistent visual grammar for the core mechanic** — excavation *fills lines*; gaps are *unfilled lines*; earned value is a line *turned brass*.

Per-screen opportunities (illustrative, not prescriptive):
- **Landing** — the hero holds a line for the visitor; the live excavation field is a line they fill and watch resolve.
- **Conversation** — each surfaced answer fills a line into the brief; open threads remain quiet hairline blanks.
- **Career Brief** — the document as filled (earned, sourced) lines vs. blanks still to fill.
- **Story Score & Gap Detection** — the gaps *are* the unfilled lines; closing one turns it brass.
- **Role tailoring** — the same lines re-threaded toward a new target.

**Discipline guardrail (critical):** the motif can be pervasive as *structure* — quiet hairline rules — but **brass stays rare and earned.** Most lines are empty/hairline; a line turns brass only at a genuine milestone. If lines-everywhere starts to read as decoration or as a literal fill-in-the-blank form, pull back — it must feel editorial and meaningful, never gimmicky. This is an **exploration**: apply it where it strengthens the screen, drop it where it doesn't, so we can judge it at the gate.

## Design anchors
- **Design system: Meritous Design System** — synced to `design-system/` (tokens in `colors_and_type.css`, `components.css`, `@dsCard` preview gallery), with the machine-readable spec at `DESIGN.md` (repo root) and the human rendering at `docs/brand.html`. Build every screen on these tokens and component classes.
- **Persona role label: "Career Partner"** (Title Case; e.g. "Ames · Your Career Partner"). "Steward" may appear as incidental flavor, but "Career Partner" is the standing convention.
- Primary user context: mid-career credentialed knowledge worker, often recently laid off; desktop-first, high-focus, emotionally loaded moment.
- Viewport: Desktop-first (fully usable on mobile, but composed for a focused desktop session).
- Fidelity target: High enough to judge whether the concept is compelling — realistic content and real component treatments, not a wireframe; not a pixel-final production UI.
- Output location: `docs/concepts/screen-[n]-[name].html` — each fully self-contained, CDN fonts only.

## What these screens are NOT
These are concept screens for a commit-or-kill gate decision, not production designs. They do not represent a complete product, a full flow, or every state. They exist to answer one question: *is the excavation → re-threading → living-corpus concept, with a value-first B2C on-ramp, compelling enough to commit to Track 2?*

Product mechanics that the pre-login moment raises — how much free value before the wall, whether the anonymous answer persists to the account, the auth/paywall model — are **Phase 5** decisions, not solved here. We prototype the *surface*, and log the mechanics in the gate brief's design notes.

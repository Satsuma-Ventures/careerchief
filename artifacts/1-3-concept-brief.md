# Concept Brief: CareerChief
*Date: 2026-07-08 · Skill: 3-concept v2.2*

## What these screens need to demonstrate

The overarching experience these screens must create: **the ideal professional cofounder, applied to your career.** Someone who picks you up when you're down. Who sees what you can't see about yourself. Who speaks candidly because they're unambiguously in your corner — not performing support, but genuinely invested in a shared goal. Who knows exactly when to push for more rather than let you settle. Not a coach running a protocol. Not a tool. A partner with skin in the game.

Within that experience, three proof points must land beyond "nice UI":
1. **The methodology is the product** — the sharp, non-obvious question a blank AI wouldn't think to ask. This is the wedge; it must be visible on screen, not described in copy. And it should feel like a cofounder asking it — because they already see you more clearly than you see yourself, and they're not going to let you undersell it.
2. **The corpus is a visibly growing, user-owned asset** — the Career Brief is something that compounds and travels with you, engineering the stickiness the thesis flags as the #1 risk. Your cofounder holds your professional story as well as you do — better in some ways. This screen makes that visible.
3. **The relationship is high-touch and durable** — not a one-and-done generator. A partner who remembers you, holds your story, and can represent you even when you're not in the room — gesturing at the always-on "sports agent / representative" horizon.

These screens exist to support a **commit-or-kill gate decision**, so they must make the value legible fast — not represent a complete product.

## Screen inventory (4 screens)

### Screen 1 — Onboarding conversation (the excavation in action)
**What it shows:** The conversational onboarding mid-session. The user has given a flat, undersold description of a past role ("I managed the vendor relationships"); CareerChief responds not with generic follow-up but with a *sharp, specific* elicitation move that surfaces buried value — e.g. surfacing scope, stakes, and outcome the user assumed was obvious — and reflects back the reframed, still-truthful version. A subtle indicator shows this exchange being captured into the growing corpus.
**Why this screen:** This is *the* differentiator — and the moment where the cofounder relationship is most viscerally felt. The question didn't come from a protocol. It came from someone who already sees the user more clearly than they see themselves, and isn't going to let them undersell it. It proves the encoded methodology ("knowing what to ask") against the "blank ChatGPT can't do this" claim. If this screen isn't compelling, nothing else matters.
**Key elements:** Conversation thread (second-person voice, no chatbot energy), the elicitation question styled with candid authority — confident and direct, not bubbly, not clinical. An inline "captured to your brief" affordance. Calm, high-focus layout. **No** progress-bar gamification or "AI is typing ✨" ornament. The tone is: your cofounder just asked "wait — what was the actual impact there?"

### Screen 2 — The Career Brief (the living, owned corpus)
**What it shows:** The primary output artifact — a living document capturing the user's narrative, strengths, and positioning, assembled from the conversation. Each element is **traceable** back to what the user said (a visible "grounded in your own words" cue). It reads as a considered, editorial document the user *owns and can export*, not a form. A quiet cue signals it keeps growing over time and can later represent the user to inbound opportunity (the "agent"/representative horizon).
**Why this screen:** Your cofounder holds your professional story as well as you do — better in some ways. They can walk into any room and represent you, even when you're not there. This screen makes that tangible: a living document that compounds, travels with you, and speaks on your behalf. It's the stickiness answer the thesis demands be designed in from the first screen.
**Key elements:** Editorial document layout (serif headings, generous space), strengths/positioning sections, traceability cues that visibly anchor every claim to the user's real words. An explicit **user-owned / exportable** affordance. A subtle "living / always-on" framing — the document grows as the relationship deepens. Gold reserved for a genuine milestone marker only.

### Screen 3 — Story Score & Gap Detection (the value-creation delta)
**What it shows:** The signal metric reflecting narrative coherence/completeness, broken into sub-dimensions (narrative clarity, keyword alignment, impact quantification), paired with **Gap Detection** — the specific delta between what the user has actually done and how they're currently presenting it, with concrete, honest prompts to close each gap.
**Why this screen:** This is the cofounder's candor made visible. Not flattery — never "you're 98% perfect!" — and not harshness. The honest read from someone genuinely invested in your outcome: "here's where you're leaving value on the table, and here's what I actually see when I look at your history." The gap list is the cofounder holding a mirror. Demonstrates the core value mechanism as *excavation, not generation* — naming what's missing and undersold rather than fabricating.
**Key elements:** Score card (the one disciplined use of gold — an achievement moment when earned, not decorative), sub-dimension breakdown, a prioritized gap list with specific, grounded next actions. Tone is candid and direct — the kind of feedback that only lands because you trust the source is in your corner.

### Screen 4 — Role tailoring & interview prep (re-threading for the target level)
**What it shows:** The user points the Career Brief at a specific target role; CareerChief re-threads the *same real history* into targeted outputs — a tailored résumé angle and role-specific interview prep grounded in the user's own stories, positioned for the level they're reaching for (not the one they're leaving).
**Why this screen:** A cofounder knows your whole history. When you say "I'm going after this," they reach into what they already know about you and aim it — the same real stories, reframed for where you're headed. "You've done this before, just in a different context." Proves re-threading, closes the excavation → outcome loop, and shows the corpus paying off repeatedly — the compounding-relationship behavior that separates a subscription that stays from a one-and-done transaction.
**Key elements:** Target-role input/context, tailored output grounded in cited real experience, interview-prep questions tied to the user's actual stories. A "same story, aimed higher" framing — the cofounder who helps you see your own history through the lens of where you're going. Outputs visibly traceable — never generic template filler.

## Design anchors
- Design system: CareerChief Design System (Claude Design project) — see `artifacts/1-3-design-system-brief.md` for the full brand sentiment and design direction.
- Primary user context: mid-career credentialed knowledge worker, often recently laid off; desktop-first, high-focus, emotionally loaded moment.
- Viewport: Desktop-first (fully usable on mobile, but composed for a focused desktop session).
- Fidelity target: High enough to judge whether the concept is compelling — realistic content and real component treatments, not a wireframe; not a pixel-final production UI.

## What these screens are NOT
These are concept screens for a commit-or-kill gate decision, not production designs. They do not represent a complete product, a full flow, or every state. They exist to answer one question: *is the excavation → re-threading → living-corpus concept compelling enough to commit to Track 2?*

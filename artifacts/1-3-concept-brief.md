# Concept Brief: CareerChief
*Date: 2026-07-08 · Skill: 3-concept v2.2*

## What these screens need to demonstrate

Someone seeing these screens should immediately understand the one thing that makes CareerChief different from every résumé tool and from a blank ChatGPT box: **it knows what to ask.** The concept must make the *excavation → re-threading* loop visible — the product surfacing real, specific value the user had dismissed as trivial, then re-threading that real history into outputs for the level they're targeting, with every claim traceable to something the user actually said.

Three things must land beyond "nice UI":
1. **The methodology is the product** — the sharp, non-obvious question a blank AI wouldn't think to ask. This is the wedge; it must be visible on screen, not described in copy.
2. **The corpus is a visibly growing, user-owned asset** — the Career Brief is something that compounds and travels with you, engineering the switching-cost/"career builder" stickiness the thesis flags as the #1 risk.
3. **The relationship is high-touch and durable** — it should feel like a senior coach in your corner who remembers you, not a one-and-done generator — and gesture at the always-on "representative/agent" horizon.

These screens exist to support a **commit-or-kill gate decision**, so they must make the value legible fast — not represent a complete product.

## Screen inventory (4 screens)

### Screen 1 — Onboarding conversation (the excavation in action)
**What it shows:** The conversational onboarding mid-session. The user has given a flat, undersold description of a past role ("I managed the vendor relationships"); CareerChief responds not with generic follow-up but with a *sharp, specific* elicitation move that surfaces buried value — e.g. surfacing scope, stakes, and outcome the user assumed was obvious — and reflects back the reframed, still-truthful version. A subtle indicator shows this exchange being captured into the growing corpus.
**Why this screen:** This is *the* differentiator. It proves the encoded methodology ("knowing what to ask") against the "blank ChatGPT can't do this" claim. If this screen isn't compelling, nothing else matters.
**Key elements:** Conversation thread (coach/user turns, second-person voice), the elicitation question styled with authority (not a bubbly chatbot), an inline "captured to your brief" affordance, calm high-focus layout. **No** progress-bar gamification or "AI is typing ✨" ornament.

### Screen 2 — The Career Brief (the living, owned corpus)
**What it shows:** The primary output artifact — a living document capturing the user's narrative, strengths, and positioning, assembled from the conversation. Each element is **traceable** back to what the user said (a visible "grounded in your own words" cue). It reads as a considered, editorial document the user *owns and can export*, not a form. A quiet cue signals it keeps growing over time and can later represent the user to inbound opportunity (the "agent"/representative horizon).
**Why this screen:** Makes the corpus-as-moat tangible and portable — the stickiness answer the thesis demands be designed in from the first screen. Shows the product's memory and the "chief of staff who's done the homework" feel.
**Key elements:** Editorial document layout (serif headings, generous space), strengths/positioning sections, traceability cues, an explicit **user-owned / exportable** affordance, a subtle "living / always-on" framing. Gold reserved for a genuine milestone marker only.

### Screen 3 — Story Score & Gap Detection (the value-creation delta)
**What it shows:** The signal metric reflecting narrative coherence/completeness, broken into sub-dimensions (narrative clarity, keyword alignment, impact quantification), paired with **Gap Detection** — the specific delta between what the user has actually done and how they're currently presenting it, with concrete, honest prompts to close each gap.
**Why this screen:** Demonstrates the core value mechanism as *excavation, not generation* — naming what's missing and undersold rather than fabricating. The score gives a legible sense of momentum (a retention hook) and is the one earned place gold appears.
**Key elements:** Score card (the one disciplined use of gold — an achievement moment), sub-dimension breakdown, a prioritized gap list with specific, grounded next actions. Honest framing — never inflated, never "you're 98% perfect!" flattery.

### Screen 4 — Role tailoring & interview prep (re-threading for the target level)
**What it shows:** The user points the Career Brief at a specific target role; CareerChief re-threads the *same real history* into targeted outputs — a tailored résumé angle and role-specific interview prep grounded in the user's own stories, positioned for the level they're reaching for (not the one they're leaving).
**Why this screen:** Proves "re-threading," closes the loop from excavation to outcome, and shows the corpus paying off repeatedly across roles — the compounding-relationship behavior that separates a subscription that stays from a one-and-done transaction.
**Key elements:** Target-role input/context, tailored output grounded in cited real experience, interview-prep questions tied to the user's actual stories, a "same story, aimed higher" framing. Outputs visibly traceable — never generic template filler.

## Design anchors
- Design system: CareerChief Design System (Claude Design project) — locked identity per `artifacts/1-3-design-system-brief.md`.
- Primary user context: mid-career credentialed knowledge worker, often recently laid off; desktop-first, high-focus, emotionally loaded moment.
- Viewport: Desktop-first (fully usable on mobile, but composed for a focused desktop session).
- Fidelity target: High enough to judge whether the concept is compelling — realistic content and real component treatments, not a wireframe; not a pixel-final production UI.

## What these screens are NOT
These are concept screens for a commit-or-kill gate decision, not production designs. They do not represent a complete product, a full flow, or every state. They exist to answer one question: *is the excavation → re-threading → living-corpus concept compelling enough to commit to Track 2?*

01

Brand foundation.

Less “AI productivity tool,” more the smartest person in your corner. Meritous is a chief of staff for your career — invested in your success, never in the spotlight.

Name

Meritous. A chief of staff for your career — a trusted partner working behind the scenes, fluent in your story, ready when an opportunity is.

Tagline

Your career chief of staff.

Mission

Help job seekers articulate the experience they already have — not invent new bullet points. The system surfaces gaps, sharpens narrative, and shows up prepared for every conversation.

Positioning

Meritous sits between resume builder and career coach — grounded enough to be trusted with the truth of your career, attentive enough to know what’s missing. The work is collaborative. Everything is built from what you actually did.

Tone of voice

Authoritative but warm. Speaks like a senior advisor who has read every line of your resume — direct, specific, never showy. Lift comes from accuracy, not adjectives.

  • Always second person — “you,” “your.” Never “users” or “candidates.”
  • Sentence case everywhere. ALL CAPS is reserved for the kicker eyebrow.
  • Em dashes ( — ) with spaces, in place of parentheses for asides.
  • Name what the feature actually does — “Surface gaps in your story,” not “AI-powered story analysis.”
  • No exclamation points. No emoji in product or marketing.

Logomark.

A geometric, faceted crown — five points constructed from angular triangular segments with a diagonal cut through the base. The wireframe is locked; only the color treatment moves within the palette.

Usage rules

  • Use the full lockup as the primary brand expression — nav, headers, hero.
  • The mark alone is for product chrome and dense footers, once context is established.
  • Never recolor with gold — gold is reserved for achievement moments only.
  • Minimum size: 20px tall in product chrome, 14px in dense footers.
  • Maintain clear space equal to the height of the mark on all sides.

Usage principles

  • Navy is the dominant structural color — nav, headlines, primary buttons, dark moments.
  • Steel is used confidently — links, secondary CTAs, accent fills, progress bars.
  • Gold is used scarcely — score numbers, milestone badges, and the 22 × 1.5px kicker rule.
  • Off-white is the default page background. Never pure white.
  • No gradients as primary backgrounds. The brand reads as solid and grounded.
  • All borders derive from navy at 10% — never neutral gray.

Typographic principles

  • Headlines set in DM Serif Display, 400 weight. Italic for warmth — one italic word per headline, never two.
  • Body copy in Plus Jakarta Sans, generous line height (1.65 — 1.70).
  • Sentence case everywhere — the kicker eyebrow is the only place uppercase appears.
  • Letter-spacing on display sizes is tight (−0.024em); body stays at default.
  • Never use the display serif below 22px — it loses its character.

Layout rules

  • Hero padding tops out at 72px. Section blocks are at least 48px apart.
  • Card padding sits at 24 — 32px. Inner stat tiles use 14 — 16px.
  • Single-column hero, max content width 1200px, comfortable side margins (≥56px desktop).
  • Two-column splits favor 60 / 40 content/imagery — never 50 / 50.
  • Sticky top nav 64px tall, off-white at 85% opacity with 12px backdrop blur.

Motion principles

  • All easings are plain ease. Spring or overshoot curves are off-limits.
  • Buttons darken or fill on hover. No scale-down transforms — the brand should not feel toy-like.
  • Cards lift via shadow step-up (sm → md). No translateY.
  • Modal backdrops are flat (no blur). Sticky headers use 12px backdrop blur at 85% opacity.

08

Visual principles.

The brand reads as solid and grounded. Where most career tools lean bright and toy-like, Meritous leans editorial — quiet, considered, adult.

Voice — yes & no

Yes “Three roles you haven’t told a story about yet.”
“I noticed a gap between your 2021 PM role and the strategy work you described — want to talk through it?”
Button: “Review my story”
No “🚀 Unlock your career potential with AI!”
“Our AI detected a discrepancy in your timeline. Click to optimize.”
Button: “GET STARTED NOW”

What to avoid

  • Gradient backgrounds as primary surfaces — the brand is solid, not iridescent.
  • Emoji in product UI or marketing copy. Bullets (•) and arrows (→) are the ceiling.
  • Pure white. The page background is always off-white.
  • 2019 SaaS verbs — “unlock,” “supercharge,” “elevate,” “transform.”
  • Leaning on “AI” as a noun in marketing. Name what the feature actually does.
  • Glassmorphism. Noise overlays. Inner shadows. Heavy drop shadows.
  • Bounce, spring, or overshoot motion. The brand should not feel toy-like.

Imagery

  • Photography appears sparingly. When it does: full-bleed, cool-toned, lightly desaturated — editorial, not stock.
  • No background images by default. The signature texture is negative space.
  • No repeating patterns or decorative textures.
  • No hand-drawn illustrations or sticker-style decoration.

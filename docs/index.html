A résumé flattens a decade of judgment into one forgettable line.

You've been in the room when it could have gone the other way, made the call no one else could, held something together when it was fraying. Written down, all of it collapses into "managed vendor relationships" — not false, just far smaller than the truth. We're too close to our own work to see its weight.

Most AI makes that worse, averaging everyone into the same competent paragraph. Your partner is an AI built the other way around: it starts from your history alone and works only in your direction — asking, listening, asking again — until the specific, true you is on the page. You direct it; it amplifies what was already yours.

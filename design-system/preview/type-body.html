The reading experience

Lead paragraphs are set in Literata — the serif carries editorial gravitas into the reading itself, not just the headings. It signals a considered, credible voice the moment the eye lands.

Beneath the lead, Literata holds sustained body copy at 18px on a 1.7 line height — a generous measure for a focused, lean-in session where someone is thinking hard about their own story. It reads as considered, not clinical; direct without being cold. Italic is reserved for genuine emphasis and for the words drawn from your own history, never for decoration.

The user's real words are set in serif italic and anchored to their source — evidence rendered as a considered quotation, not machine output.
